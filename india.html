Natural Resources in India

India is rich in various natural resources, including forests, minerals, water, sunlight, and more. Here are some of the key resources found in India:

1.Forests

Moist Tropical Forest a. Tropical wet evergreen: Western Ghats (Maharashtra, Karnataka, Kerala).
b. Tropical semi evergreen: Lower hills of western Ghats.
c. Tropical moist deciduous: Dehradun, mahableshwar.
d. Damp Forests: Surder bans, Bengal delta, and Andaman

2.Minerals

India is a leading producer of various minerals such as coal, iron ore, manganese, bauxite, and mica.

  • Coal: India is the world's second-largest producer of coal, which is a major source of energy for the country.
  • Mica: India is one of the largest producers of mica, which is used in electrical and electronic industries.
  • Bauxite: India has significant reserves of bauxite, which is the primary ore for aluminum production.

3.Water

India has a vast network of rivers, lakes, and groundwater resources that are crucial for agriculture, drinking water, and industrial use.

4.Sunlight

India receives abundant sunlight throughout the year, making it ideal for harnessing solar energy.

5.Rocks

India has a rich variety of rocks used in construction, mining, and as natural resources.

6.Animals

India is home to diverse wildlife, including iconic species like the Bengal tiger, Indian elephant, and peacock.

7.Soil

India's soil varies from region to region, supporting a wide range of agricultural activities and crop production.

8.Air

In India wind energy play crucial and vast role for energy production
